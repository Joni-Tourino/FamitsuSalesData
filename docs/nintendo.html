Nintendo dominated the charts both on consoles and games

by Jonathan Touriño Jacobo

The Nintendo Switch had another great year

The console reached 15 million units sold, and Ring Fit Adventure passed the 2 million mark

Without a single doubt, Nintendo's latest game in Animal Crossing's franchise was the game of the year, at least sales wise. Tom Nook and company were able to beat long standing records set by the biggest franchise in Japan: Pokemon. And to explain some of them, what better way to do it inside the world of Animal Crossing: New Horizons.


  • The release of the Lite version keeps boosting sales of the Nintendo Switch who passed 5,5 M in its last month of the year. The base line has steadily sold a similar amount every year: 3,4 million units.
  • In November the Switch already passed the total from 2019, and if it keeps the pace of 200,000 consoles sold, for the remaining three weeks, it'll end the year with more than 6 million consoles sold in Japan. Despite having stock issues during February and at the beginning of March.
  • Nintendo released 9 games on its hybrid console for 2020, with Animal Crossing: New Horizons being its biggest success in years. The launch week of Animal Crossing allowed for the console to have its best week ever, with more than 390,000 units sold between the two models. Better even than the launch week of the Nintendo Switch, with 330,000.
  • The lack of competition in the market, mainly due to a scarcity of stock for the PlayStation 5 around the world, and strong sales from Animal Crossing puts, currently, the console as the 9th most sold in Japan. And with 90,000 units of difference with the Super Nintendo, it'll end 2020 in 8th position.
  • Counting both the Nintendo Switch and Lite models, 2020 will make it the third best year for a console, behind the Nintendo DS' performances in 2006 and 2007.

    Only Switch games in the top 30?

    In the second week of December, out of the 30 best retail games, 28 were for the Nintendo Switch, while the remaining two for the PlayStation 4. A first in the past 20 years of Famitsu's charts.

    And this may not be the last time we'll see a list were almost all (or even all) games are for a single console. Depending on how well Cyberpunk 2077 (PS4) will do in the coming weeks, the year may end up with only Nintendo Switch titles in the top 30, and almost half of them from Nintendo and The Pokemon Company.
