The year of Animal Crossing

by Jonathan Touriño Jacobo

Already at six million units sold, most weeks at number one and many more records for the game

Without a single doubt, Nintendo's latest game in Animal Crossing's franchise was the game of the year, at least sales wise. Tom Nook and company were able to beat long standing records set by the biggest franchise in Japan: Pokemon. And to explain some of them, what better way to do it inside the world of Animal Crossing: New Horizons.



Sources for the numbers and other stats: Famitsu, Ludostrie and Game Data Library.
Nintendo's numbers come from their second quarter financial report. As stated, those numbers do include digital sales, which means that by end of September, the game had managed to sell around 2,4 million copies on the Nintendo eShop. Almost one in three games were then sold digitally.


Other stats to explain the success of the game

Animal Crossing New Leaf, for the Nintendo 3DS, was the best selling game in the franchise. Worldwide sales put it close to 12,5 million copies sold. With Japan's sales alone, New Horizons is currently 4,4 million (counting Nintendo's numbers) away from the previous game.


Animal Crossing had the best first week for a game on the Nintendo Switch. It also was the best first week for any Nintendo game that is not Pokemon. Without digital sales, the journalist Oscar Lemaire wrote on Twitter that Famitsu's numbers puts it as the 10th best first week since 1995. Pokemon Black/White (2010), for the Nintendo DS, still holds the record with more than 2,6 million games sold in 2010.

According to Daniel Ahmad, a senior analyst at Niko Partners, the game had exceeded 2,5 million units in its first three days, counting the digital sales too. That would put Animal Crossing at the same level as of Final Fantasy VIII's 2,5 million games sold for the PlayStation 1, back in 1999.

New Horizons became the fifth game in passing the six million mark in physical sales in Japan. The other games to do so are:

  • Pokemon Red/Blue/Green for the Game Boy
  • Pokemon Gold/Silver for the Game Boy
  • Super Mario Bros. for the NES
  • New Super Mario Bros. for the Nintendo DS

Most of Nintendo's top franchises (Pokemon, Mario and even Zelda) have had decent second years with the Nintendo Switch. Ranging between one million to half a million. Due to the success of Animal Crossing: New Horizons in 2020, it'd be safe to say that the game may at least sell more than 500,000 copies in 2021.
