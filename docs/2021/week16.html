Highlights of the week:

  • For the first time since November, a PlayStation 4 game takes the first spot. Which according to the Twitter account @ChartsJapan is five weeks better than the previous record, from the PlayStation.

    • Interesting fact: in the last Famitsu weekly chart, NieR Replicant PS4 was #1 and break a run of 22 consecutive first places for Nintendo Switch games.

    It is the longest run ever (since 1995), ahead from the 17 weeks of PS1 in 1999.

    Here is the full top 10: pic.twitter.com/hLk1IeIktg

    — Video Games Charts (@ChartsJapan) April 30, 2021

  • Square Enix's Nier Replicant didn't manage to outperform NieR Automata but it did better than the original version.

    • Retail launch week sales for the new NieR: Replicant in Japan are the second biggest for series in Japan.

    Higher than the combined launches of the original Replicant and Gestalt, but expectedly below that of Automata.

    Great launch for the game. pic.twitter.com/h3AXsUK4lp

    — Game Data Library (@GameDataLibrary) April 29, 2021

  • Most Nintendo games saw a bump in sales, with Ring Fit Adventure increasing more than 30%.
