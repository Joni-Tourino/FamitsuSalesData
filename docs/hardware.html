Nintendo Switch kept sales of consoles steady

by Jonathan Touriño Jacobo

Lifetime for the Nintendo Switch is at 17 million in Japan

Nine out of ten consoles sold this year, was a Nintendo Switch. By itself, the Switch passed, by a few thousands, the total of all machines sold in 2019. Lifetime sales of the Nintendo Switch passed 17 million in 2020. For a fourth year in a row, it's the best selling console and keeps selling more than the previous one.
Sony and Microsoft launched their next generation of consoles in November (Week 46); The PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series S|X, respectively. First week for the PlayStation 5 was shy of 120,000, while the Xbox Series totaled 20,000 consoles sold. Both Sony and Microsoft have faced difficulty to meet the demands worldwide since the launch of their new consoles.
The best week (12) for the year coincided with the release of Animal Crossing: New Horizons with more than 400,000 machines sold, with nineteen out of every twenty being a Switch.

The launch of the PlayStation 5 was only better than the one of the PlayStation 3. A higher than expected demand, caused by the pandemic, has had Sony unable to meet supply worldwide. This explains the low numbers since its launch in Japan.
The transition to a new generation, and the lack of stock for the PlayStation 5, leaves Sony under the million units sold between PlayStation 4 and 5's totals. It's only the second time this happened to Sony.
The previous time it happened was in 1994, when Sony ventured in the videogame business and launched the PlayStation during the first week of December and ended the year with 280,000 consoles sold. If we don't count the handheld systems, the PS Vita and the PSP, we'd have to go back to 2013, when the PlayStation 3 sold less than 850,000 units.

Aside from the original Xbox, Microsoft was never able to fully enter the Japanese videogame market, which explains the low numbers for the Xbox One and Xbox Series launch week. Similar to the PlayStation 5, The Series S|X also suffered stock problems worldwide.
